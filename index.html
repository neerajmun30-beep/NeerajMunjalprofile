About Me

With 13 years of experience in program management, I have led large-scale development initiatives across Rajasthan, Punjab, Himachal Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Haryana, and Chhattisgarh, focusing on health, education, nutrition, gender, and water security. I currently serve as a Senior Program Manager at the Piramal Foundation in Chhattisgarh, where I guide multi-state strategies and on-ground implementation.

My work centers on designing and executing innovative, cost-effective programs rooted in qualitative research and data analysis. I collaborate closely with state and local governments to strengthen systems, improve service delivery, and enable community-driven change. A key focus of my work is advancing gender equity alongside improvements in health and education through Panchayati Raj Institution‚Äìbased solutions.

Over the years, I have contributed to strengthening maternal and child health services; managing community-based severe acute malnutrition (C-SAM) initiatives; implementing the Incremental Learning Approach (ILA) for nutrition and health; and promoting foundational literacy & numeracy, gender-inclusive education, and socio-emotional & ethical learning (SEEL).

I have supported Gram Panchayat Development Plans (GPDPs), strengthened Gram Panchayat Planning Facilitation Teams (GPPFTs), and aligned Village Water & Sanitation Committees with Jal Jeevan Mission priorities. By mentoring a diverse team of more than 30 professionals, I drive capacity building, foster government partnerships, and strengthen sustainable, community-led development models that prioritize inclusive participation of women and marginalized groups.

Committed to long-term systemic transformation, I aim to build scalable, Panchayat-led models where communities take ownership and lead lasting progress across health, education, nutrition, water security, and gender equity.
